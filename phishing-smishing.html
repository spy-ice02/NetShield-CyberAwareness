Phishing & Smishing

Master the art of deception and learn how attackers use emails, SMS, and fake portals to harvest your sensitive data.

The Art of Deception

Phishing and Smishing (SMS Phishing) revolve around impersonation. Attackers send highly believable messages masquerading as trusted institutions (banks, government, couriers) to induce panic or urgency. Their goal is almost always to make you click a malicious link and manually input your credentials, credit card details, or OTPs on a counterfeit website.

The Golden Rule

Verify the source independently. NEVER click on links within unexpected urgent messages!

How Scams Work (Modus Operandi)

The four most common techniques scammers use right now to steal your identity and money.

The Electricity Cut-off Threat

"Dear Customer, your electricity will be disconnected tonight at 9 PM due to bill non-payment. Please click here to update your details." Clicking leads to a fake portal designed to extract your banking credentials and OTP.

Fake KYC Suspension

Scammers send SMS messages claiming your bank account or PAN card KYC has expired, and your account will be frozen within 24 hours. The provided link meticulously replicates the official bank's login page.

Courier or Customs Fraud

You receive an email or SMS stating a package cannot be delivered due to incomplete address or unpaid customs fees (a tiny amount). You are asked to pay ₹10 via card, exposing your CVV and OTP.

Reward or Lottery Claims

Emails claiming you've won a smartphone or significant cash prize. To claim it, you are led to a form demanding extensive personal information or asked to pay a "processing fee" via a fraudulent gateway.

Ultimate Prevention Guide

Follow these strict rules to immunize yourself against social engineering.

  • Do Not Click Link Directly

    If you receive an alert from your bank regarding an apparent issue, open your browser and manually type your bank’s secure address. Never simply click the provided link.

  • Check the URL Thoroughly

    Look for subtle misspellings (e.g., `hdfc-update.com` instead of `hdfcbank.com`). Even minor deviations indicate a completely fraudulent portal.

  • Beware of Urgency Hacks

    Attackers prey on emotion. Legit agencies like banks or electricity boards do not send immediate “act within 2 hours or face suspension” SMS messages without proper protocols.

  • Never Share OTPs Intended for Others

    If an OTP arrives unexpectedly, it means someone is attempting to log in. Under no circumstances should you provide it to an incoming caller.

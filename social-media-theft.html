Educational Guide

Social Media Identity Theft

Fraudsters target your digital persona. By stealing information you casually share online, they can perfectly impersonate you or completely commandeer your social accounts to scam your entire network.

The Digital Doppelgänger

Phishing and Smishing (SMS Phishing) revolve around impersonation. Attackers send highly believable messages masquerading as trusted institutions (banks, government, couriers) to induce panic or urgency. Their goal is almost always to make you click a malicious link and manually input your credentials, credit card details, or OTPs on a counterfeit website.

The Golden Rule

Protect your digital footprint. Always verify urgent money requests via a direct voice call!

How Scams Work (Modus Operandi)

The four most common techniques scammers use right now to steal your identity and money.

Profile Cloning

Scammers copy your photos and create an identical fake profile. They then message your friends lists asking for small amounts of money due to a fabricated emergency.

OTP Interception

You receive a message from a 'friend' saying they accidentally sent their login OTP to your number. Once you share it, your WhatsApp is immediately hacked.

Romance Scams

Scammers spend months building trust and emotional connection through dating apps before concocting an elaborate story requiring immediate financial help.

Fake Investment Gurus

Hackers take over a legitimate friend's account and post screenshots of massive crypto profits, tricking you into 'investing' on a fake portal.

Ultimate Prevention Guide

Follow these strict rules to immunize yourself against social engineering.

  • Lock Your Profile

    Ensure your photos, posts, and friend list are restricted strictly to 'Friends Only'. Never keep your friend list public.

  • Enable 2-Step Verification

    Activate 2FA with an authenticator app (like Google Authenticator) on all email and social media accounts.

  • Never Share OTPs

    Never forward a 6-digit SMS code to anyone, even if they claim to be a close friend or family member.

  • Verify Emergency Requests

    If a contact asks for financial help abruptly via text, literally call them on the phone and speak to them directly.

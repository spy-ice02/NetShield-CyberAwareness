UPI & Payment Frauds

Understanding exactly how scammers exploit the system, and the single golden rule to protect your hard-earned money.

The Core Reality of UPI Scams

The UPI infrastructure itself is highly secure. Scammers rarely "hack" the app or the bank server. Instead, they use Social Engineering (Mental Manipulation) to create panic, urgency, or greed, tricking the user into willingly handing over control or authorizing payments.

The Golden Rule

Entering your UPI PIN is ONLY required for SENDING money. You will NEVER need to enter your PIN to receive money.

How Scams Work (Modus Operandi)

The four most common techniques scammers use right now to drain your accounts.

QR Code Scam (OLX/Quikr)

On marketplaces, a fraudster poses as a buyer, agrees to your price, and sends a QR code via WhatsApp. They claim scanning it will transfer funds into your account. In reality, scanning it initiates a debit from your account to theirs.

Screen Sharing Traps

Scammers pose as bank or app support and guide you to install apps like AnyDesk or TeamViewer. Once installed, they see your screen live, send an OTP to your phone, read it off your screen, and complete fraudulent transactions.

'Collect Request' Trap

You get a notification on GPay or PhonePe saying you've won a cashback. It's actually a "Collect Request" initiated by the scammer. The screen says "Enter PIN to receive reward." Doing so gives them the money instead.

Fake Customer Care

Scammers manipulate Google search results or Twitter tags to list their personal numbers as official customer support for Swiggy, Paytm, or Banks. When a genuine victim calls for help, they are smoothly routed into a refund scam.

Ultimate Prevention Guide

Follow these strict rules to immunize yourself against social engineering.

  • Never Enter PIN to Receive

    Treat your UPI PIN exactly like an ATM PIN. You don't enter your ATM PIN to take a deposit. The same logic applies to UPI. If the keypad appears, money is leaving your account.

  • Block Screen Sharing Promptly

    Never download AnyDesk, TeamViewer, QuickSupport, or similar apps on the instruction of anyone on a call, no matter how official they sound.

  • Use In-App Official Support Only

    Do not Google customer care numbers. Only use the 'Help & Support' section directly inside your official banking or service application to raise disputes.

  • Read the Warning Screen

    GPay and PhonePe display large, red warnings when you are about to send money to an unknown contact. Do not ignore these system warnings out of a sense of urgency.

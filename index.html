Key Insights

Recent data indicates that financial frauds constitute the vast majority of cybercrimes reported in the country. Let's break down the primary threats:

  • Financial Frauds (~77.5%)

    Includes UPI scams, debit/credit card fraud, and phishing links. It remains the most prevalent threat targeting victims' savings directly.

  • Task/Job Frauds (~12.0%)

    A rapidly growing scam where victims are lured with partial payments for liking videos or reviewing products online.

  • Social Media & Identity (~10.5%)

    Predatory loan apps, digital arrests, and profile impersonation designed to socially engineer and extort money from victims.

Educational Hub

Stay aware of the most common modern scams and protect your digital identity.

UPI & Payment Frauds

Scammers impersonate buyers and send a QR code or PIN request, claiming it's to "send" you money. Scanning it deducts funds from your account.

Protection Tip: You only need your UPI PIN to SEND money, never to receive it. Don't scan unknown QRs.
Read Deep Dive Hub

Phishing & Smishing

You receive urgent SMS alerts claiming your bank account KYC is suspended or your electricity will be cut off, with a dangerous link attached.

Protection Tip: Never click links in unexpected panic-inducing messages. Contact official customer care directly.
Read Deep Dive Hub

Social Media Identity Theft

Fraudsters clone your profile picture and name to create a fake account. They then message your friends asking for emergency financial help.

Protection Tip: Lock your profile, hide your friend list, and always verify emergency requests via a direct phone call.
Read Deep Dive Hub

Task & Part-Time Job Scams

Scammers offer simple jobs like "liking YouTube videos" or "reviewing hotels" on Telegram. They pay initial small sums, then demand a deposit for a "premium task" and steal your money.

Protection Tip: Never pay money to get a job. If someone asks for a "deposit" to unlock high-paying tasks, it's a scam.
Read Deep Dive Hub

Digital Arrest

Fraudsters impersonate CBI, Police, or Customs officials over a video call, claiming illegal items were found in a parcel in your name. They demand a "security deposit".

Protection Tip: Indian agencies NEVER digitally arrest citizens via Skype/WhatsApp or ask for money to dismiss charges.
Read Deep Dive Hub

Predatory Loan Apps

Unlicensed third-party loan apps offer quick cash but secretly steal your contact list. They later use morphed photos to extort huge repayments.

Protection Tip: Only borrow from RBI-registered NBFCs. Deny unnecessary app permissions to your contacts and gallery.
Read Deep Dive Hub

What to do if you have been scammed?

01

Block Access

Immediately freeze your bank accounts, credit cards, and change passwords for breached accounts to prevent further loss.

02

Call 1930

Call the National Cyber Crime Helpline (1930) immediately within the golden hour to block fraudulent transactions.

03

Report Online

File an official, detailed electronic complaint on the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal (cybercrime.gov.in).

04

Save Evidence

Take screenshots of chats, payment receipts, bank statements, and fake emails before the scammer deletes them.
